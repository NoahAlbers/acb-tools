Where the 30% goes

Buy, hold & sell costs — roughly 10–15% of ARV. Two sets of closing costs, loan interest and points, property taxes, insurance and utilities while you hold, and the agent commission when you sell.

Target profit — roughly 15–20% of ARV. Your pay for the risk, the capital, and months of work. Most experienced flippers won’t take a project that pencils below this.

Add those together and you get ~30% — that’s why the rule discounts ARV by 30% before repairs even enter the math.

The 70% rule is a screen, not an underwriting model. It shines on mid-priced flips; on very cheap or very expensive homes, fixed costs distort the percentage — always follow up with a full cost workup. Planning to rent instead of flip? Run the numbers in our Rental Property ROI Calculator.
