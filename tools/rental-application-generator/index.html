Screening tips for landlords
  • ✓Write your criteria down before you advertise — minimum income-to-rent ratio, credit floor, rental-history requirements — and apply them identically to every applicant. Consistent, documented criteria are your best fair-housing defense and lead to better tenants.
  • ✓Know your FCRA duties. If you deny an applicant, require a co-signer, or raise the deposit based even partly on a credit or background report, you must send an adverse-action notice that names the reporting agency, gives its contact information, and explains the applicant's right to dispute and get a free copy of the report.
  • ✓Never ask about protected classes — race, color, religion, sex, familial status, disability, or national origin — and check state and local law for additions like source of income, age, marital status, or sexual orientation. Screen the application, not the person.
