Normal wear & tear vs. damage — know the difference
✓ Normal wear — NOT deductible
  • Matted or lightly worn carpet
  • Small nail holes from hanging pictures
  • Faded or dingy paint
  • Minor scuffs on walls and floors
  • Loose grout, worn fixtures from ordinary use
✗ Damage — deductible
  • Pet stains, burns, or rips in carpet
  • Large holes in walls or doors
  • Broken fixtures, windows, or appliances
  • Unauthorized paint or alterations
  • Excessive filth requiring deep cleaning

Deducting for normal wear and tear is the #1 thing that loses deposit disputes — and many states award the tenant double or triple damages for a wrongful withholding. When in doubt, document with photos and attach receipts or invoices for every deduction.
